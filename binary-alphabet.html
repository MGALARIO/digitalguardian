Binary Alphabet

EVERY LETTER IS 7 BITS
Every letter you type — whether uppercase A or lowercase z — is stored by a computer as a 7-bit binary code. That means just seven 0s and 1s can represent all 128 characters including all English letters (big and small), numbers, and symbols. This system is called ASCII (American Standard Code for Information Interchange). Press any letter on the keyboard below to watch the signal travel through the wires!
